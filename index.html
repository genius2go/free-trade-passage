✓ Go direct — Cape May → Nantucket
  • Settled high pressure over the Northeast
  • SW or W winds under 15 kts for the day
  • Seas under 4 ft offshore
  • No NE on the horizon for 24+ hrs

Palm Beach → St. Augustine — City Marina
Day 1 — all outside · depart at a civilized hour · ~8 hrs running
~185 nm · well within range
📋 Capt. Rick’s Notes
St. Augustine City Docks · (904) 825-1026
About an 8-hour run from Ft. Lauderdale at cruise speed — call ahead for your slip. Downtown location, easy walking to everything.
The Gulf Stream runs close to shore between Palm Beach and Cape Canaveral. With any NE wind it stacks up fast — check conditions before you leave. Ft. Pierce (~110 nm) is a natural mid-leg contingency if you want to break it up or conditions aren’t ideal. St. Augustine bar: cross at or near high tide — work backwards from tide tables when planning your departure.

Southport → Morehead City → Coinjock — overnight 🥩
Day 4 · Masonboro Inlet outside to Morehead · then inside via Neuse River → Pamlico → Alligator River → Coinjock
~110 nm · call ahead for the prime rib
📋 Capt. Rick’s Notes
Northbound from Southport: up the ICW toward Wrightsville Beach, then exit via Masonboro Inlet to Morehead City. After Morehead, inside via Neuse River to the outer banks, through the bridges, Currituck Sound, and on to Coinjock.
Pamlico Sound is a non-event on a 55-footer in normal conditions. Depart Morehead City at a reasonable hour and cross before afternoon winds build. The only scenario worth pausing for is a strong NE blowing 20+ knots. Coinjock is tonight’s destination — call ahead and order the prime rib before you arrive.
📋 Capt. Rick’s Notes — Coinjock overnight
Coinjock Marina · (252) 453-3271 — dock is on the east side of the channel — restaurant is right there.
Call ahead and order the prime rib before you arrive — it won’t be ready if you don’t. Two sizes: 16 oz and 32 oz.
⚠️ Tip the dock hands in cash — do not tip on the credit card machine.

Depart Coinjock at 6:05 AM running 26 knots to make the North Landing Bridge at 7:30 AM. Monitor VHF Channel 13 for all bridge openings.

Norfolk → Cape May — South Jersey Marina
Outside preferred · exit Cape Henry · run NE up the coast past Delmarva · enter Cape May Inlet from the ocean · Chesapeake City/C&D Canal if offshore is rough
~185 nm outside · comfortable full day at cruise speed
📋 Capt. Rick’s Notes
Outside is Rick’s preferred route — and the right call on a 55-footer with a good window.

South Jersey Marina, Cape May · (609) 884-2400
“Outside is good” — excellent staging marina for the final leg. Good fuel dock, easy approach. Enter from the ocean side. Top off completely here before deciding anything about the Nantucket run.
⚖️ Outside via Cape Henry (preferred) or inside via Chesapeake City / C&D Canal
▶ Outside — exit Cape Henry (Rick’s route)

Exit Hampton Roads via Cape Henry, pass the Chesapeake Light Tower, run NE up the coast past the Delmarva Peninsula, and enter Cape May Inlet directly from the ocean on the east side. ~185 nm, a comfortable full day. You want SW or W winds under 15 kts, seas under 4 ft.

▶ Inside — up the Bay · Chesapeake City · C&D Canal (if offshore is rough)

Head north up the Chesapeake Bay to Chesapeake City (~100 nm), overnight or fuel stop, then through the C&D Canal into Delaware Bay, run south to Cape May and enter from the bay/canal side (~80 nm more). Longer but fully protected. Delaware Bay also chops up with NE winds.

Cape May → Nantucket — Direct or via Montauk 🏁
Direct ~225 nm · or Cape May → Montauk (~130 nm) then Montauk → Nantucket (~105 nm)
~225 nm direct · ~8–9 hrs at cruise · well within range
With Free Trade’s range, fuel is not the question — weather is. At cruise speed, ~225 nm is roughly an 8–9 hour run. Depart Cape May at a normal hour and you’re in Nantucket by mid-afternoon. Montauk is a completely legitimate option — 130 nm there, overnight, then a relaxed 105 nm finish the next morning with a fresh look at Nantucket Sound conditions.
⚖️ Direct to Nantucket or stage at Montauk — decide in Cape May
▶ Cape May → Nantucket direct

Exit Delaware Bay, round the tip of NJ, run NE offshore past Fire Island and Block Island into Nantucket Sound. ~225 nm, ~8–9 hrs. Depart at a normal hour, arrive mid-afternoon. You want settled W or SW, seas under 4 ft, no NE on the horizon.

▶ Cape May → Montauk → Nantucket

~130 nm to Montauk, a comfortable day run. Fuel up, sleep, finish with a fresh 105 nm leg. Nantucket Sound with afternoon SW against ebb builds fast — time arrival for before midday.
