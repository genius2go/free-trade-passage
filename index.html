Fort Pierce City Marina
~110 nm from Palm Beach · Natural mid-leg bailout if the Gulf Stream stacks up with NE wind. Often called the best all-weather inlet on the East Coast. Downtown, walking to restaurants, easy in/out. (772) 464-1245
📅 Book on Dockwa
Around Fort Pierce — things to do
🍽 Restaurants — walking distance
Cobb’s Landing right at the marina — live music, ask the marina office for the boater discount coupon. 2nd Street Bistro on the waterfront. Sailfish Brewing Co. for craft beer. 12A Buoy for seafood. Manatee Cafe nearby for breakfast/lunch.
🌧️ Weather hold — what to do
National Navy SEAL Museum on Hutchinson Island is the only one of its kind — half-day visit, short Uber. Saturday morning Downtown Farmers Market is one of the best in Florida. Manatee Observation Center if you’re here in winter (free, manatees in the warm-water discharge). Walkable historic downtown.
📍 About this place
Founded 1838. The “Treasure Coast” name comes from a 1715 Spanish fleet that sank off this stretch — gold and silver still wash ashore after storms. The Fort Pierce Inlet has been called the best all-weather inlet on the East Coast.
Picks from Dockwa reviews + local knowledge.
Around St. Augustine — things to do
🍽 Restaurants — walking distance from City Docks
O.C. White’s right across the street (classic Florida seafood, has been there forever). Harry’s a block north toward the fort (Creole/Cajun, courtyard seating). The Floridian for Southern. Columbia Restaurant on St. George St. for Spanish/Cuban — the oldest restaurant in Florida.
🌧️ Weather hold — what to do
Castillo de San Marcos is a 5-minute walk — Spanish stone fort from 1672, oldest masonry fort in the U.S. Stroll pedestrian St. George Street (shops, restaurants, galleries). Flagler College tour (former Ponce de Leon Hotel, Tiffany glass). Lightner Museum just across the bridge. Ghost tours after dark are surprisingly good.
📍 About this place
Founded by the Spanish in 1565 — the oldest continuously inhabited European settlement in the United States. 450+ years of seafaring history. The Bridge of Lions next to your slip opened in 1927.

Brunswick Landing Marina — Brunswick, GA
~130 nm from St. Augustine · Easy mid-leg bailout. Called the “Velcro Marina” — once you arrive, you don’t want to leave. Strong liveaboard community, free laundry, walking to downtown. (912) 262-9264
📅 Book on Dockwa
Around Brunswick — things to do
🍽 Restaurants — walking distance
Cooter Brown’s is the Dockwa-cruiser favorite. Many downtown restaurants offer 10% off for marina guests. Indigo Coastal Shanty for fusion. Marshside Grill for Southern. Marina hosts complimentary happy hour Mon/Wed/Fri with the cruising community (free beer on tap, bacon nights).
🌧️ Weather hold — what to do
First Friday street festival if it lines up with your stay (food trucks, art, vendors). Mary Ross Waterfront Park. Old Town Brunswick Historic District. Marina has free bikes — explore the town easily. Day trips: Jekyll Island (driveable, beaches and historic district), St. Simons Island, Cumberland Island National Seashore.
📍 About this place
Founded 1771. Former U.S. Navy hurricane hole during WWII — that’s why insurance companies love it (most won’t insure boats south of here during hurricane season). Locally known as the “Velcro Marina” — once you arrive, you don’t want to leave.
Picks from Dockwa reviews.
Harbour Town Yacht Basin — Hilton Head Island, SC 📋 In Rick’s notes
~150 nm from St. Augustine · Weather bailout or fuel stop. Sea Pines Resort marina, multiple restaurants, walking to Calibogue Sound. Harbourmaster Leslie. (843) 363-8335 · Great food at the Country Club, walking distance (Rick)
📅 Book on Dockwa
Around Hilton Head — things to do
🍽 Restaurants
Great food at the Country Club, walking distance — Rick means the Sea Pines Country Club. On-site at Harbour Town: Links (American grill overlooking the golf course), Coast (waterfront seafood), Fraser’s Tavern (casual), The Quarterdeck (on the harbor).
🌧️ Weather hold — what to do
Walk up the iconic red-and-white Harbour Town Lighthouse for the view (90 ft, small museum inside). Sea Pines Forest Preserve has trails. Beaches via shuttle or bike. Excellent bike paths throughout Sea Pines — rentals at the marina. The Harbour Town Golf Links is home of the RBC Heritage PGA Tour event.
📍 About this place
Sea Pines was the original master-planned resort community on the East Coast (1956). Harbour Town’s design was inspired by Portofino, Italy. The lighthouse isn’t a working navigational aid — it was built as a marketing feature in 1969 and became one of the most photographed lighthouses in the world.
Country Club from Rick’s notes. Other restaurants from Dockwa marina listing.
Around Charleston — things to do
🍽 Restaurants — walking, or short Uber
Walking from the marina to Market St. takes about 2 miles (~$10 Uber). Husk for elevated Southern, Halls Chophouse for steaks, FIG for the Charleston food-scene experience. Publix is a ½ mile walk for provisioning.
🌧️ Weather hold — what to do
Walk the Battery and look at the antebellum mansions. Rainbow Row. Charleston City Market. Patriots Point with the USS Yorktown across the bridge in Mount Pleasant (Uber). Fort Sumter ferry from Liberty Square.
📍 About this place
Founded 1670. The Civil War started here when Confederate batteries fired on Fort Sumter in 1861. The historic district is largely intact — many buildings predate the Revolution.

Around Southport — things to do

Around Morehead City — things to do
🍽 Restaurants — walking distance
Marina is in historic downtown — over 20 restaurants within walking distance. The Ruddy Duck is the popular pick from cruisers. Sanitary Restaurant (yes, that’s really the name) is the local institution — been there since 1938, classic NC seafood on the waterfront.
🌧️ Weather hold — what to do
Walk across the bridge to Beaufort (about 1 mile) — it’s the older, more interesting town. NC Maritime Museum on Front St. is excellent (Blackbeard’s anchor and cannon from Queen Anne’s Revenge are on display). Atlantic Beach is a short Uber for sand and surf. Bring quarters — the marina rents a $20/2-hr courtesy car for provisioning runs.
📍 About this place
Working waterfront, founded 1857. Hemingway used to stay here. Original home of the Big Rock Blue Marlin Tournament (still runs every June). The yacht basin sits on the prime locale picked nearly a century ago — first marina on the Crystal Coast.

Around Coinjock — things to do
🍽 Restaurants
The Coinjock Marina Restaurant is essentially the only game in town — and that’s a feature, not a bug. Home of the famous 32oz prime rib since 1978. Local seafood too. Call ahead or you won’t get the prime rib.
🌧️ Weather hold — what to do
Honestly — there isn’t much. Coinjock is the marina, plus a hardware store, barbershop, and post office. If you’re stuck more than a night, rent a car (Norfolk is an hour north). On the upside: the new marina store is well-stocked, bathrooms are clean, and there’s live music Fridays in season.
📍 About this place
ICW Mile Marker 50. The name comes from the local indigenous word for the berries that still grow here. The marina has been a transient yachtsman favorite for decades — if you’re going north or south on the ICW and not taking the Dismal Swamp route, you’re breaking your run here.

📋 Capt. Rick’s Notes — Norfolk overnight
Waterside Marina · (757) 625-3625📅 Book on Dockwa
Down town and close to all restaurants. New floating docks (2020), 20 ft depth, complimentary grocery shuttle. Right at ICW Mile Marker 0 on the Elizabeth River.
Around Norfolk — things to do
🍽 Restaurants — walking distance
~60 restaurants in the Waterside District steps from the slip. Try The Monastery (authentic Eastern European, a short walk worth making) per Dockwa cruiser tips. The Waterside District building itself has casual options — can get loud with live music on summer weekends.
🌧️ Weather hold — what to do
Nauticus & the Battleship Wisconsin are two blocks from the marina — the USS Wisconsin is a WWII-era battleship you can walk the decks of. Chrysler Museum of Art is a 10-min walk (free, with a working glass-blowing studio). MacArthur Memorial. Slover Library. NEON arts district. Ferry across to Portsmouth is short.
📍 About this place
Home of the world’s largest naval base. Hampton Roads is one of the deepest natural harbors on the East Coast. Heavy Navy and commercial traffic in the channel — they always have right of way. Monitor VHF 16.

⚖️ Cape May (primary) or push 30 nm to Atlantic City
▶ Cape May — South Jersey Marina

The classic stop. Lobster House right next door. Excellent staging for the Nantucket leg. Books up fast in season — reserve well in advance.

▶ Atlantic City — ~30 nm past Cape May

If South Jersey is full, or conditions are great and you want to position closer to Nantucket. Add ~1 hr at 30 kts. Two marina options — Golden Nugget (big basin, casino restaurants) or Kammerman’s (quieter, family-run, newly rebuilt).

Atlantic City option — Golden Nugget or Kammerman’s
Golden Nugget — Farley State Marina, Atlantic City 📋 In Rick’s notes
~30 nm past Cape May (~1 hr at 30 kts) · Big-marina option. 630 slips, well-protected basin, casino & restaurants right there (Chart House, Vic & Anthony’s). Functional but a bit worn. (609) 441-8482
📅 Book on Dockwa
Around Atlantic City — things to do
🍽 Restaurants — in the Golden Nugget complex
Chart House and Vic & Anthony’s Steakhouse right at the marina (both highly rated, harbor views). Red Sushi in the casino. Off-property: Knife & Fork Inn is the local steakhouse institution (short Uber).
🌧️ Weather hold — what to do
Golden Nugget casino is right there if that’s your thing. H2O Rooftop Pool. The Atlantic City Boardwalk is 5 miles long — Boardwalk Hall (former Miss America) and Steel Pier amusement area are at the far end (about 3 miles from the marina, take the Jitney shuttle). Absecon Lighthouse is climbable (228 steps).
📍 About this place · ⚠ safety note
One of America’s oldest seaside resorts. Inspiration for the original Monopoly board (street names included). Prohibition-era rum-running history — this marina basin was where the rumrunners offloaded. Local advice from cruisers: stay near the marina, casino, and boardwalk after dark; avoid wandering side streets.
Picks from Dockwa reviews.
Kammerman’s Atlantic City Marina
~30 nm past Cape May · Quieter family-run alternative. Just rebuilt — 50 floating slips, two 180′ fuel docks, vessels up to 200′. On Absecon Inlet near the USCG Station. ~1 mi to the Boardwalk via Uber. Owners Stacey & Chris Kammerman live on site. (609) 348-8418 · after-hours: (609) 513-1818 (Stacey)

Around Atlantic City — things to do
🍽 Restaurants
On-site: Sirens Bar & Grill, private to the marina, owner-run. ~1 mi to the Boardwalk by Uber for more options — Chart House & Vic & Anthony’s at the Golden Nugget. Knife & Fork Inn is the local steakhouse institution (short Uber).
🌧️ Weather hold — what to do
Uber to the Boardwalk (5 miles long) — Boardwalk Hall (former Miss America), Steel Pier amusement area, Absecon Lighthouse (climbable, 228 steps). Casinos if that’s your thing.
📍 About this place · ⚠ safety note
Family-owned since 1961 — Kammermans live on site. Recently rebuilt facility (50 slips, two 180′ floating fuel docks, high-speed dispensers). Located near the USCG Station, quieter than the Golden Nugget basin. Local advice from cruisers: stay near the marina or use Uber after dark; avoid wandering side streets.

▶ Outside — exit Cape Henry

Exit Hampton Roads via Cape Henry, run NE up the Delmarva coast, enter Cape May Inlet from the ocean on the east side. ~185 nm, comfortable full day. SW or W winds under 15 kts, seas under 4 ft.

North up the Chesapeake Bay to Chesapeake City (~100 nm), through the C&D Canal into Delaware Bay, run south to Cape May and enter from the bay/canal side. Longer but fully protected.

🍽 Restaurants — walking distance from the marina

O.C. White’s right across the street (classic Florida seafood). Harry’s a block north toward the fort (Creole/Cajun, courtyard seating). The Floridian for Southern. Columbia Restaurant on St. George St. for Spanish/Cuban — the oldest restaurant in Florida.
🌧️ Weather hold — what to do
Castillo de San Marcos is a 5-minute walk — Spanish stone fort from 1672, oldest masonry fort in the U.S. Pedestrian St. George Street (shops, restaurants, galleries). Flagler College tour (former Ponce de Leon Hotel, Tiffany glass). Lightner Museum just across the bridge. Ghost tours after dark are surprisingly good.
📍 About this place
Founded by the Spanish in 1565 — the oldest continuously inhabited European settlement in the United States. 450+ years of seafaring history. The Bridge of Lions next to the slip opened in 1927.

Fort Pierce City Marina — Fort Pierce, FL
~110 nm from Palm Beach (~75 nm south of St. Augustine) · Last bailout before the Gulf Stream / Palm Beach approach. Often called the best all-weather inlet on the East Coast. Downtown, walking to restaurants. (772) 464-1245

Around Fort Pierce — things to do
🍽 Restaurants — walking distance
Cobb’s Landing right at the marina — live music, ask the marina office for the boater discount coupon. 2nd Street Bistro on the waterfront. Sailfish Brewing Co. for craft beer. 12A Buoy for seafood. Manatee Cafe nearby for breakfast/lunch.
🌧️ Weather hold — what to do
National Navy SEAL Museum on Hutchinson Island is the only one of its kind — half-day visit, short Uber. Saturday morning Downtown Farmers Market is one of the best in Florida. Manatee Observation Center if you’re here in winter (free, manatees in the warm-water discharge). Walkable historic downtown.
📍 About this place
Founded 1838. The “Treasure Coast” name comes from a 1715 Spanish fleet that sank off this stretch — gold and silver still wash ashore after storms. The Fort Pierce Inlet has been called the best all-weather inlet on the East Coast.
